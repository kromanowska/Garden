Instructions

Performance evaluations are intended to measure the extent to which the employee’s performance meets the requirements of a particular position and to establish goals for the future; strengthen the relationship between you and the employee; open up channels of a communication; appraise past performance; recognize good performance; identify areas that might require improvement; enable you to access your own communication and supervisory skills.

Listed on the following pages are a number of performance factors that are important in the successful completion of most assignments. A list of qualities has been included to assist in evaluating an employee’s performance in each area. To complete the form, choose a mark representing the level of achievement, which most accurately describes the employee’s performance on each factor.

Definition of terms

Consistently below expectations
Performance is at a level below established objectives with the result that overall contribution is marginal and substandard. Performance requires a high degree of supervision.
Below expectations
Meets some of the established objectives and expectations but definite areas exist where achievement is substandard. Performance requires somewhat more than normal degree of direction and supervision.
Meets expectations
Meets established objectives in a satisfactory and adequate manner. Performance requires normal degree of supervision.
Exceeds expectations
Accomplishments are above expected level or essential requirements.
Consistently exceeds expectations
Job performance easily exceeds job requirements; performance approaches best possible attainment.

Knowledge

  1. Understands job procedures and equipment essential to job
  2. Stays up to date on job methods, skills, and techniques
  3. Understands job functions within the organizational structure

Quality

  1. Produces a final work product that is accurate and complete
  2. Corrects errors and recognizes inconsistencies in work assigned
  3. Organizes work to make duties easier and the supervisor’s job easier
  4. Maintains awareness of changes in technical areas and responds to those changes
  5. Achieves results consistent with job expectations and performance standards

Quantity

  1. Provides acceptable amount of work
  2. Accomplishes volume of work efficiently and promptly
  3. Meets job requirements and produces results within established time limits
  4. Uses available work time effectively, plans and prioritizes work, sets and accomplishes goals, and completes assignments on schedule
  5. Maintains output level consistent with job expectations and performance standards

Task and project management

  1. Develops and meets priorities, schedules, and deadlines
  2. Maintains an effective level of service/activity in assigned areas
  3. Develops methods and procedures in fulfilling daily tasks and projects
  4. Monitors quality control and work completion within given level of resources

Dependability

  1. Arrives on time for scheduled work
  2. Changes schedule/plans in order to meet deadlines
  3. Accomplishes all tasks within the proper time frame
  4. Completes work thoroughly, eliminating the need for close review
  5. Demonstrates general knowledge of the supervisor’s work and department functions
  6. Applies knowledge so matters are attended to/referred to appropriate person for action
  7. Puts forth extra effort when needed

Adaptability / stress tolerance

  1. Adjusts to changes in job assignments, methods, personnel or surroundings
  2. Meets deadlines or handles several tasks simultaneously
  3. Demonstrates flexibility to adapt to unanticipated schedule changes
  4. Manages stress, flexible, accepts constructive comments, and demonstrates improvement

Initiative / resourcefullness

  1. Contributes suggestions and ideas or develops options
  2. Seeks out new and better ways of accomplishing tasks
  3. Identifies and applies available information and resources
  4. Generates effective ideas and solutions
  5. Seeks additional tasks as time permits
  6. Performs work without being told

Judgment / Decision making

  1. Evaluates several responses to a problem
  2. Considers impact of alternatives
  3. Ensures decisions are made and/or referred to appropriate administrative level
  4. Takes responsibility and makes decisions within assigned authority
  5. Uses good judgment to arrive at logical conclusions
  6. Demonstrates the ability to take time action

Departmental policies and procedures

  1. Follows departmental policies and procedures
  2. Completes forms in a proper manner keeping current on changes in department policies and procedures
  3. Recognizes and informs appropriate parties of non-compliance of department policies and procedures

Employee development and goal setting

  1. Sets personal and professional goals
  2. Achieves the desired results, purpose or work-related goals
  3. Initiates and provides suggestions for job enrichment and expanded duties

Relationships with people and communication

  1. Uses tact to diffuse difficult situations
  2. Offers help to others when time permits
  3. Responds effectively and courteously to others
  4. Contributes to improve the level of employee morale
  5. Addresses and resolves conflict/problem situations with others
  6. Works and communicates effectively with coworkers and supervisors
  7. Receives, comprehends and disseminates oral and written communication
  8. Develops and maintains a positive relationship
